§ 04 — The Guide

Captain Cody Christensen.

A Coast Guard–licensed guide and lifelong student of Rainy Lake's moods — the water, the weather, and the fish that read both.

“What I love the most about guiding is watching a client catch the biggest fish of their life. I love the smiles on their faces.”

Cody has guided on Rainy Lake and the Rainy River for thirty-two seasons. He is a Coast Guard–licensed Captain running a 2021 Ranger 1880 with a 200 hp Yamaha, top-of-the-line electronics, and Fenwick rods paired with President reels — all provided.

He specializes in walleye, bass, crappie, northern, and muskie, and runs the spring sturgeon program on the Rainy River. Whether you're new to the water or a lifelong angler, the day ends the same way.

§ 07 — The Place

Rainy Lake & River.

Two-hundred-twenty-thousand acres of public water — straddling the line between Minnesota and Ontario.

The lake holds more than 2,500 islands, coldwater structure, and some of the continent's most durable trophy fisheries. In spring the Rainy River runs thick with sturgeon and walleye; by midsummer the smallmouth and muskie light up the rocks.
